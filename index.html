What does CNP mean?

A logo that makes invisible evidence visible.

The CNP logo is designed to help consumers recognize natural cosmetic products whose scientific information is communicated clearly and responsibly.

It brings attention to what really matters: what the product claims, which ingredients support those claims, whether the finished formulation has been tested, and what safety and quality information is available.

The logo is not meant to say “trust us.” It is meant to show people what they can trust — and why.

What CNP brings to light

The science consumers rarely see on the label.

CNP is designed to make the following areas easier to identify, understand and compare.

Claims

The evidence behind product claims

What supports the promises made by the product, and how relevant that evidence is to the final formulation.

Ingredients

The scientific role of active ingredients

What the main active or functional ingredients are expected to do, explained in clear and accessible language.

Finished product

Available tests on the finished product

Whether the complete cosmetic formulation was evaluated, rather than relying only on evidence about individual ingredients.

Quality

Stability, quality and formulation data

Information about formulation stability, product quality and the technical documentation available.

Safety

Safety information, allergens and warnings

Relevant precautions, allergens, usage instructions and other information that supports appropriate product use.

Performance

Claim-specific evaluations

Tests related to moisturising, antioxidant, anti-age, soothing, barrier-support or other product-specific effects.

How the concept works

From technical files to a clearer consumer message.

No dashboards, no scientific jargon on the package. The goal is to review the relevant information and translate it into a simple transparency signal people can recognize.

Step 1

The brand shares its evidence

Claims, ingredients, finished-product tests, quality data and safety-related information are gathered for review.

Step 2

CNP organizes what matters

The available information is assessed and translated into a clear, consumer-friendly explanation.

Step 3

The logo signals transparency

The CNP mark helps the customer recognize that the science behind the product has been communicated more clearly.
